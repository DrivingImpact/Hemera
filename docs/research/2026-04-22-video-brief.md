## Script Structure

### Opening (15 sec) — The Problem
**Founder 1, to camera:**

"Every organisation has a carbon footprint. The problem is, most don't know what theirs actually is — and the ones that try to measure it end up with a spreadsheet full of guesses and no idea how accurate any of it is."

### The Insight (15 sec) — Why Current Tools Fail
**Founder 1, continues:**

"Existing tools give you a number. We think that's dangerous. A single number without a confidence interval is like a financial forecast without a margin of error — it looks precise, but it's misleading."

### Introducing HemeraScope (20 sec) — What We Do
**Founder 2, to camera:**

"HemeraScope is a carbon intelligence platform. You give us your spend data — invoices, bank exports, Xero CSVs — and we give you back a carbon footprint where every single figure has a confidence interval, every supplier has been verified against public registries, and every calculation is traceable to its source."

### How It Works (45 sec) — Screen Recording
**Voiceover (either founder), showing the product:**

1. **Upload** — "Upload your spend data in any format. CSV, Excel, even Xero exports. Our parser recognises 50+ common column formats automatically."
   *(Show: upload dropzone, data type picker)*

2. **Classify & Calculate** — "We classify every transaction using DEFRA's official conversion factors — the same ones the UK government uses for its own reporting. Every factor is traceable to a specific row in a government-published spreadsheet."
   *(Show: QC card with emission factor, click Verify to show the DEFRA table modal)*

3. **Quality Control** — "Every report goes through ISO 19011-inspired sampling. An analyst reviews a statistically significant sample of transactions. You see exactly what was checked and what passed."
   *(Show: QC card flow, pass/fail swipe)*

4. **Supplier Intelligence** — "We don't just calculate carbon. We check every supplier against 13 public data sources — Companies House, Environment Agency, sanctions lists, modern slavery registers, government contracts. If something's wrong, we find it."
   *(Show: supplier detail page with enrichment layers expanding)*

### The Differentiator (20 sec)
**Founder 2, to camera:**

"We call it the Glass Box approach. No black-box calculations. Every figure, every finding, every risk flag is backed by evidence you can see and verify. We believe sustainability data should be as trustworthy as financial data."

### Social Proof (10 sec)
**Founder 1, to camera:**

"We're working with UK students' unions to prove the model. The early results have been [specific — e.g. 'eye-opening', 'they found suppliers they didn't know were dissolved', whatever the real story is]."

*(Replace with real quote/anecdote when available)*

**Both founders, to camera:**

"If you want a carbon footprint you can actually trust — with confidence intervals, source traceability, and supplier intelligence built in — get in touch. We're taking on new engagements now."

## Production Notes

### Equipment (low budget)
- iPhone 15/16 Pro in 4K — perfectly adequate
- Natural light (window behind the camera, not behind the subject)
- Lapel mic or AirPods Pro (avoid room echo)
- Clean, minimal background (office wall, bookshelf, or branded backdrop)

### Screen recordings
- Use macOS screen recording or OBS
- Record at 1920x1080 minimum
- Use the actual product with real (anonymised) data, not placeholder/dummy data
- Zoom in on the parts that matter (QC cards, emission factor modal, supplier enrichment)

### Editing
- Simple cuts, no fancy transitions
- Add captions/subtitles (accessibility + social media)
- Add HemeraScope logo watermark in corner
- Background music: subtle, corporate, royalty-free (e.g. Artlist, Epidemic Sound)
- Colour grade to match brand: warm, clean, teal accents if possible

### Distribution
- Embed on landing page (replace current placeholder)
- YouTube (for SEO — "UK carbon footprint software", "ESG reporting tool")
- LinkedIn (both founders' profiles + company page)
- Cut a 30-second version for social media

## Key Messages to Hit

1. **Confidence intervals, not just numbers** — this is the #1 differentiator
2. **Government-grade data sources** — DEFRA factors, Companies House, Environment Agency
3. **Glass Box transparency** — everything is traceable
4. **Supplier intelligence included** — not just carbon, but the full supply chain picture
5. **Built for UK organisations** — UK-specific data, UK legal frameworks, UK registries

## What NOT to say

- Don't claim certifications you don't have (no "ISO certified", "GHG Protocol certified")
- Don't mention AI prominently — buyers are sceptical. Say "analyst-reviewed" not "AI-powered"
- Don't overpromise on accuracy — the whole point is honesty about uncertainty
- Don't trash competitors by name

Our Approach

Hemera's supplier intelligence methodology combines structured data collection with expert analysis to build a comprehensive picture of each supplier's ESG performance. Our analysis draws on regulatory databases, public filings, certification registries, and expert analysis across 8 weighted ESG domains.

Assessment Domains

Each supplier is assessed across eight domains, weighted to reflect their relative importance to supply chain resilience and regulatory compliance:

DomainFocus Areas
Governance & IdentityCorporate registration, filing history, director due diligence, legal standing
Labour & EthicsEmployment practices, Modern Slavery Act compliance, working conditions
Carbon & ClimateEmissions reporting, carbon commitments, energy performance
Water & BiodiversityEnvironmental permits, pollution incidents, resource management
Product & Supply ChainProduct safety, certifications, upstream traceability
Transparency & DisclosureFinancial reporting, ESG disclosures, public accountability
Anti-CorruptionSanctions screening, political exposure, bribery risk indicators
Social ValueCommunity impact, diversity commitments, social enterprise credentials

The Hemera Score

The Hemera Score is a weighted composite (0–100) reflecting a supplier's overall ESG standing. Domain scores are combined using weights calibrated to UK regulatory priorities and client-sector relevance. A confidence indicator (high, medium, low) accompanies each score, reflecting the breadth and recency of underlying data.

Data Sources

Our analysis draws on a wide range of authoritative sources including Companies House filings, Environment Agency registers, Health & Safety Executive data, certification bodies, sanctions databases, and specialist ESG data providers. Each data point is timestamped, versioned, and traceable to its source.

Collaborative Process

Supplier intelligence is not a one-time exercise. Hemera monitors registered data sources on an ongoing basis and proactively engages with suppliers to verify findings, request additional information, and support improvement. This report represents a point-in-time assessment — scores and findings will evolve as new data emerges and suppliers respond to engagement.

Methodology details are available on request. Hemera's scoring framework is reviewed annually and aligned with evolving UK regulatory requirements including the Modern Slavery Act, Environment Act, and forthcoming UK SRS sustainability reporting standards.

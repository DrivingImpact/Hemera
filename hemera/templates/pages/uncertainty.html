Uncertainty Analysis

Every emission estimate in this report carries a quantified uncertainty, calculated using the ecoinvent pedigree matrix methodology (Ciroth et al., 2016, The International Journal of Life Cycle Assessment, 21, 1338–1348). This approach is widely adopted in Life Cycle Assessment and provides a systematic, reproducible framework for uncertainty quantification — avoiding the arbitrary "plus or minus 20%" ranges that lack scientific basis.

The pedigree matrix scores each data point across five indicators of data quality: Reliability (how the data was measured or estimated — direct measurement scores 1, qualified estimate scores 3, rough estimate scores 5), Completeness (whether the data represents the full population of interest), Temporal correlation (how closely the emission factor year matches the reporting period), Geographical correlation (whether the factor is specific to the UK or a proxy from another region), and Technological correlation (whether the factor matches the specific process or is a sector average). Each indicator is scored on a scale of 1 (best) to 5 (worst).

These five scores are converted into a Geometric Standard Deviation (GSD) using the formula: GSD = exp(sqrt(sum of squared uncertainty contributions from each indicator)). The GSD captures the spread of the lognormal probability distribution around the central emission estimate. From the GSD, the 95% confidence interval is derived as [estimate / GSD2, estimate x GSD2]. This means a GSD of 1.5 implies the true value could be roughly half to double the central estimate at 95% confidence. Lower GSD values (closer to 1.0) indicate higher-quality data and tighter confidence intervals. The lognormal distribution is used because emission data is inherently non-negative and typically right-skewed — physical emissions cannot be less than zero.
